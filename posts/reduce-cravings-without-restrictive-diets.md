Cravings are not a sign of weakness. They are a natural response to stress, hormones, habit loops and even blood sugar fluctuations.

Here are five science-supported strategies to reduce cravings without depriving yourself.

---

## 1. Prioritize protein in your first meal

Research shows that high-protein breakfasts reduce:

- hunger hormones  

- cravings later in the day  

- blood sugar swings  

Examples:

- eggs + vegetables  

- Greek yogurt + berries  

- protein oatmeal  

---

## 2. Don't let yourself get "over-hungry"

Long gaps between meals can trigger strong cravings.

Try to:

- eat structured meals  

- include protein + fiber  

- keep snacks balanced (fruit + protein)

---

## 3. Stay hydrated

Mild dehydration is often mistaken for hunger.  
Water, tea or mineral water can help reduce unnecessary eating.

---

## 4. Improve sleep quality

Poor sleep increases ghrelin (hunger hormone) and reduces leptin (satiety hormone).

Better sleep = fewer cravings.

---

## 5. Use mindful eating techniques

Slow down:

- breathe between bites  

- remove distractions  

- taste the food intentionally  

This reduces the "urge loop".
